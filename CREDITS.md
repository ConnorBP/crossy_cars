The procedural WAV files are original generated assets, produced with Python's standard-library `wave`, `math`, `struct`, and `random` functionality:

- `assets/audio/ambient.wav`
- `assets/audio/click.wav`
- `assets/audio/coin.wav`

They do not contain third-party recordings or samples.

### Generated reward, penalty, and tire scrub effects

These effects are generated by `tools/generate_audio.py`, a
self-contained standard-library Python synthesiser (no third-party packages).
Re-running the generator reproduces byte-identical output via a fixed random
seed (`20260712`) and pure math, so the script is its own provenance.

- `assets/audio/positive.wav` -- a short, bright, **rising** reward chime: an
  ascending major triad C5 -> E5 -> G5 -> C6 (~523 -> 1047 Hz) with bell-like
  inharmonic partials, a soft attack, exponential decay, and an octave shimmer
  tail. Mono, 16-bit, 44100 Hz, ~0.46 s, normalised to ~90% of full scale. It is
  deliberately distinct from `coin.wav` (a single stable ~1316 Hz tone): it
  sweeps *upward* in pitch and is brighter.
- `assets/audio/penalty.wav` -- a short, low, **downward** dull impact: a
  phase-continuous pitch glide descending from ~330 Hz to ~110 Hz over ~0.30 s
  with a fast low-frequency onset thud and muted (fundamental-heavy) character.
  Mono, 16-bit, 44100 Hz, ~0.30 s, normalised to ~90% of full scale. It is
  deliberately distinct from `hit.wav` (a single-ish ~1118 Hz tone): it is
  lower, descends, and is duller.
- `assets/audio/tire_scrub.wav` -- a 0.48-second softened, low-passed noise and
  tonal tire scrub used at different gains/pitches for hard turns and sustained
  handbrake drifts. Generated with seed `20260716`; mono PCM16, 44100 Hz.

All files are peak-normalised with headroom and contain no clipped samples,
keeping them web-safe and level-consistent with the rest of the audio set. The
generator only writes these three files and explicitly refuses to overwrite the
existing six assets.

## Models and textures

Roady combines procedural geometry and textures with validated authored GLB models. The optional miniature surface-detail library under `assets/textures/pbr_detail/` contains 256px transformed derivatives of user-supplied, free-to-use PBR maps. `assets/textures/pbr_detail/manifest.json` records every source archive/entry, source and output SHA-256, channel packing, attenuation, truncation-recovery policy, and generated output size. The original 2K/4K sources are not shipped.

The selected sources are:

- `scuffed-plastic-1-bl.zip`
- `concrete_wall_01_4k.zip`
- `wood_01_4k.zip`
- `whispy-grass-meadow-bl.zip`
- `Smudges01_4K.png`

The user confirmed these source materials are free to use. Roady preserves its procedural colors and toy-town finishes; the derivatives supply only restrained normal, roughness, and ambient-occlusion modulation.
